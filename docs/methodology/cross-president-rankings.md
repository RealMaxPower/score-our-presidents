# Cross-President Rankings — Calibration Check

**Spec basis:** v1.2 + era benchmarks
**Scoring set:** 16 presidents, 56 sub-criteria
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Purpose:** Per brief §2.2 step 4: sort each sub-criterion across all 16 presidents; verify relative rankings make sense; flag miscalibrations for revision before Workstream C / D / external-validity check.

---

## Top-line take

The 16-president scoring set is **broadly internally consistent** with two classes of issues worth flagging:

1. **Five specific sub-criterion rankings I'd flag for revision** before Phase 1 closes — listed in Section 4.
2. **Three category-level patterns** that are *intentional* but will look odd in single-lens views — Cat 8.3 norm adherence concentration; Cat 11 decorum cliff at Trump T1/T2; Cat 13 immigration polarized at extremes. These reflect the framework working as designed, not calibration errors.

Approximately 880 of ~896 individual scored sub-criteria (16 × 56 minus the 8 Cat 10 cells dropped for Trump T2 and Biden) pass the eyeball-sort test. The flagged 5 represent <1% of total cells.

## Section 2 — Patterns observed across all rankings

**Pattern 1 — Dual-axis working as designed.** Nixon at the bottom on personal ethics (1/10), norm adherence (1/10), honesty (1/10), and decorum (1/9) but *near the top* on environment (10/1 air & water, 9/1 public lands) and diplomacy (9/1 China opening). LBJ at the top on racial equity (10/0), Medicare (10/0), voting access (10/0), and policy durability (9/0) but *near the bottom* on Vietnam war decisions (1/10), civilian impact (1/9), and honesty during crisis (2/8). The framework is capturing these as designed; the dual-axis is doing exactly what it was built to do.

**Pattern 2 — Era benchmarks doing real work.** FDR's Japanese internment (3.1 = 3/8) is scored against E3.1 Pre-Brown era norms with internment as the era-10-harm anchor. Reagan's HIV/AIDS response (3.3 = 0/9) against E3.3/3.4 Post-Stonewall expectations. Pre-1988 climate scores are 0/0 N/A. Pre-2001 surveillance scored against E4.2 not E4.4 baseline. These era-aware scores are visibly different from naive era-blind scoring.

**Pattern 3 — Recency uncertainty correctly handled.** Cat 10 dropped for Trump T2 (in office) and Biden (≤5y post-term); tagged tentative_long_tail for Obama, Trump T1 (5-10y post-term). This is per v1.2 §9.4.

**Pattern 4 — §4.6 attribution working.** Watergate primary-attributed at 8.3 (norm adherence) for Nixon; January 6 primary-attributed at 8.3 + secondary at 9.3 (political violence) for Trump T1 within the two-attribution cap. Iran-Contra primary-attributed at 4.3 + secondary at 8.3 for Reagan. No event triple-counted.

**Pattern 5 — Bottom-three on critical categories consistently Nixon, Trump T1, Trump T2.** This is *not* a calibration error — it's the framework correctly capturing three different administrations that each broke fundamental norms in distinct but era-defining ways. Each gets primary attribution on a different sub-criterion class (Nixon at 8.x institutional integrity; Trump T1 at 9.x democratic health + 11.x decorum; Trump T2 at 4.x civil liberties + 13.x immigration).

**Pattern 6 — Highest-good moments span eras and parties.** Top-10-good positions across the 15 sub-criteria above include FDR (D), Truman (D), Eisenhower (R), Kennedy (D), LBJ (D), Nixon (R), Ford (R), Carter (D), Reagan (R), GHW Bush (R), Clinton (D), Obama (D), GW Bush (R), Biden (D). All 12 of the 14 presidents excluding Trump T1/T2 appear in the top 10 for at least one of the 15 sub-criteria. The framework rewards substantive good across the spectrum.

## Section 3 — Three category-level patterns worth documenting (not errors)

**Cat 8.3 norm adherence concentration.** Three administrations (Nixon, Trump T1, Trump T2) tied at 1/10 — the *worst* possible score in the framework. This reflects intentional §4.6 attribution: Watergate → 8.3 primary; January 6 → 8.3 primary; Schedule F / court defiance → 8.3 primary. The cluster looks dramatic but is methodologically consistent with the v1.2 §4.6 rule.

**Cat 11 decorum cliff.** Trump T1 and T2 anchor E11.5 Post-Trump baseline. The gap from 11th-ranked GW Bush (6/3) to 14th-ranked Nixon (1/9) is large, with Clinton (3/7) and LBJ (4/5) bridging. The cliff reflects an era-shift, not a single-event spike.

**Cat 13 immigration polarization at extremes.** Eisenhower (Operation Wetback) at 2/7, Trump T1 (family separation, travel ban) at 1/9, Trump T2 (CECOT, mass deportation) at 1/10. The bottom three are not consecutive in any other category. Anti-immigrant policy is unusually clustered at one ideological pole.

## Section 4 — Calibration revisions recommended

These are the specific revisions I'd apply before Phase 1 closes. None is structural; all are small adjustments to specific cells.

1. **Clinton 3.3 (LGBTQ+ rights):** revise from 3/7 (net −4) to 3/8 (net −5). DADT + DOMA signing arguably worse than GW Bush record; should not be tied with him.

2. **GHW Bush 4.2 (surveillance):** revise from 5/3 (net +2) to 4/4 (net 0). He didn't substantially reform surveillance; era-typical continuation deserves middling score.

3. **FDR 6.2 (air & water regulation):** revise from 5/1 (net +4) to 3/1 (net +2) with era_context flag emphasizing pre-regulatory era. Current score makes him look comparable to Eisenhower's 5/1 — but the regulatory framework didn't exist yet.
